About

  • January 1, 0001 5 min. read

Emmanuel-Jelome Fonseca

I'm a Software Developer at Tradebyte1 in Ansbach, Bavaria who is in a constant quest2 of self-discovery3.

Self-discoveries so far

On a personal level

  • an Agreeable and Open person
  • with an INFP-T personality
  • who has an Average IQ
  • with quite a few interests (see also other tests and pseudosciences)

On a technical level

  • a Python4 programmer at work
  • and a Ruby5 enthusiast on hobbies
  • aims to become a modern LISPer6 using Clojure7 in the future

On waking life

Mostly associated to only a few but important people in life — direct family, close friends and good colleagues. Striving to only spend time on genuine relationships and meaningful activities — as Nietzsche8 wrote:

Is life not a thousand times too short for us to bore ourselves?

Personal

Self-discovery is quite a task — requiring openness to explore the potentials and courage to accept the limitations.

Here are some of the platforms I used that might be credible enough to reveal one's identity — except that their accuracy weren't guaranteed.

Agreeable and Open

According to Understand Myself9, which evaluates personalities based on The Big Five Aspect Scales10, I lean towards Agreeableness11 (80/100, highest trait) and Openness12 (90/100, highest aspect), which suggests a positive link on entrepreneurship and creative work.

Ironically, being low in Conscientiousness13 (11/100, lowest trait) and very low in Intellect (5/100, lowest aspect) implies negative competence in administrative and repetitive tasks as I prefer leadership and team play than managerial and egotism.

The level of Neuroticism14 (50/100, balanced view) validates my neutral viewpoint in life, such that, individuals can be good or evil, happy or unhappy depending on their choices, actions and circumstances in life.

It was also a bit surprising to learn that I'm not as Introverted15 (57/100) as I thought — but still — I get fairly exhausted in crowds unless that crowd are the people I deem important. Nevertheless, parties will never be my thing.

INFP-T

According to 16Personalities16, which assess people based on Myers–Briggs Type Indicator17 (MBTI), I lean towards INFP18-T19. Note, however, that Myers–Briggs’ personality test is criticized in its scientific accuracy and even labelled as a pseudoscience20.

On the other hand, INFP-T suits introversion (I), agreeableness (__F_) and openness (_N_P) pretty well. Turbulence (-T) seems to be a recent add-on to increase variants although it's a good fit for perfectionists who self-doubts (see impostor syndrome21).

Average IQ

According to Mensa Norway22, which tests individual's cognitive abilities, I have an IQ of about 105 (which is average, so to speak). Of course, I could have achieved a higher score by means of repetition, but that will be a fool's errand, as it merely will increase my test score, not IQ.

Having said that, however, IQ, in my humble opinion, is overrated — which might also be the reason that I'm average per se — and mustn't be viewed as the end all be all of any individual.

While having higher IQ has its own merits, it shouldn't be used as an absolute measure. The fact that life treats everyone equally regardless of intelligence illustrates that. So rather than chasing intelligence and accumulatation knowledge — seek wisdom and find out the truth.

Other tests

There were also old tests that I took and somehow kept their scores:

  • Highly Sensitive Person (19/27) in SPS23
  • Mid-brained dominant (50/50) in brain dominance
  • 1% more feminine than most men in gender role24
  • Associative thinker in terms of thought process
  • Uses defensive pessimism25 as the cognitive strategy26

Pseudosciences

I also checked a few mysticism27 outside science (see noumenon28):

  • Diamond as the birthstone29 (both month and day)
  • Aries (Ram ♈), fire element, cardinal quality in western astrology30
  • Earth Dragon (Dragon animal, yang earth element) in chinese zodiac31

To be fair, they got it right in numbers (7) and colors (silver, gray).

Interests

Outside of computers, I like:

  • Art (drawing)
  • Music (flute)
  • Philosophy (metaphysics)

Technical

I find joy in constant exposure to different kinds technologies — old and new — that can provide unique perspectives to solve different problems.

At Work

As a Software Developer, we use Python4 as the programming language with Django32 as the web framework that sits on top of AWS33. Strangely enough, I have proclivity with JavaScript34 esp. when it comes to UIs.

Hobbies

At home, I intentionally use the opposing technologies on hobby projects to have different perspectives such as Ruby5 as the programming language with Ruby on Rails35 as the web framework. The combination of GitHub36 (versioning), GitHub flow37 (branching model), Travis CI38 (building) and Heroku39 (hosting) with Heroku Flow40 (CD workflow) for a complete CI/CD41 pipeline — and as an advocate of open source42, I license these hobby projects under MIT43.

On the local development, I also use different sets of tools (preferred but not intentional) to do these hobby projects such as Arch Linux44 as the operating system, i345 as the desktop environment, BASH46 as the shell, Make47 as the build tool, Spacemacs48 (an Emacs49 distribution) as the text editor and Markdown50 (for simple writing) or Org mode51 (for complex writing) for documentation.

The Future

The ideal future, that I aim, taking both personal and technical sides into account, is to become a modern LISPer6 using Clojure7 / ClojureScript52 with the main focus on user interfaces and the guiding principles of HCI53.
