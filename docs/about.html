Personal

Self-discovery is quite a task — requiring full honesty to explore the potentials and extreme courage to accept the imperfections.

Here are a few hints received from platforms that might be credible to reveal one's true identity — except accuracy isn't guaranteed.

Agreeable and Open

According to Understand Myself, which evaluates personalities based on The Big Five Aspect Scales, I lean towards Agreeableness and Openness. That is, being high on Agreeableness (80/100, highest trait) and very high in Openness (90/100, highest aspect) suggests a positive link on entrepreneurship and creative work. Ironically, being low in Conscientiousness (11/100, lowest trait) and very low in Intellect (5/100, lowest aspect) implies negative competence in administrative and repetitive tasks.

The level of Neuroticism (50/100, balanced view) confirms my neutral stand about life, such that, individuals can be good or evil, happy or unhappy, depending on their choices and circumstance in life. An Introvert (57/100) who is exhausted in crowds unless that crowd are people deemed important.

INFP-T

According to 16Personalities, which assess people based on Myers–Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI), I lean towards INFP-T.

Myers–Briggs personality test, however, is criticized in its scientific accuracy (even labelled as pseudoscience). On the other hand, INFP-T very well suits introversion (I), agreeableness (__F_) and openness (_N_P). Turbulence (-T) seems to be a recent addition to increase variants though fits perfectionists who self-doubts (see also impostor syndrome).

Average IQ

According to Mensa Norway, which tests individual's cognitive abilities, I have an IQ of 105 (average). Of course, one can achieve higher scores by means of repetition, but that is a fool's errand, as it's mere improving test scores, not IQ.

However, IQ, to me, is overrated, and must not be seen as the end all be all of any individual — although higher IQ have its own merits, it can never be the final judge. For instance, one can be exceptionally intelligent but miserable while the other is not but successful — and that's the beauty in life: it does not descriminate intelligence.

Other tests

Pseudosciences

Here are also mysticism in an effort to explore what science can't (see noumenon).

Using birthdate as the sole reference, the results were: diamond in birthstone (both month and day), Aries (Ram, symbol ♈), fire element and cardinal quality in western astrology and Earth Dragon (Dragon animal, yang earth element) in chinese zodiac.

To be fair, they got it right in numbers (7) and colors (silver and gray).

Technical

At Work

As a Software Developer at Tradebyte Software GmbH, we use Python as the programming language with Django as the web framework that sits on top of AWS cloud platform. Strangely enough, I have proclivity with JavaScript esp. when it comes to UIs.

Hobbies

At home, I intentionally use the opposing technologies on my hobby projects to see a different perspective, such as: Ruby as the programming language with Ruby on Rails as the web framework. The combination of GitHub (versioning), Travis CI (builds) and Heroku (hosting) for a complete CI/CD pipeline — and as an advocate of open source, these projects are licensed under MIT.

On the local development, I also use different sets of tools (preferred, not intentional) to do the hobby projects, they are: Arch Linux as the operating system, i3 as the desktop environment, BASH as the shell, Make as the build tool, Spacemacs (an Emacs distribution) as the text editor and Markdown (simple writing) or Org mode (complex writing) for documentation.

Ideal Future

The ideal future, that I aim, taking personal and technical sides into account, is to become a modern LISPer (Clojure), focusing on user interfaces (ClojureScript) with the guiding principles of HCI to teach the concepts and build systems of the future.
